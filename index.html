Hi, I am Shreejal.


I believe that it's the interplay of design and engineering that allows us to create transformative products. Design is more than just visual aesthetics: it is how a user interacts with a product.

People are complex.

Phase 1.


Human beings are different but what's draws us together are our similarities.

To map out behavorial traits that a group of people might exhibit, I developed the User Personas for the group. By creating multiple fictional characters representative of a potential market, we could better understand their needs as consumers.

Trying to Understand Consumer Need and Developing Concepts


Phase 1 Contd. and Phase 2


I learned about AEIOU (and sometimes Y) in elementary school - nearly a decade ago. However, I never would've presumed that the applications of AEIOU, developing POG statements and understanding the concept generation process would help us better understand people.

Our customers are the ones who use our product and if we don't attempt to understand them, our company sinks. I was at the fore-front with team members building several diagrams to envision the proper process a user might follow before preparing/ making food.

Designers need the right tool.


Phase 3


Designers enable to product to do great things. However, if they don't have the right tools, then they can't build the perfect plane.

The same way you can't build a plane without the right wings, you can't build a great product without Miro - especially over zoom. Google Docs has limited capabilities. I found that it was not meant for design. We flew over to Miro and in learning the platform, I found that it allowed me to become more detail-oriented. I will continue using Miro beyond this class.
